Case study: How does a bike-share navigate speedy success?

Welcome to my deep dive into Cyclistic, a fictional Chicago-based bike-share program on a mission to convert casual riders into annual members. In this case study, I apply the full analytics process—from gathering and cleaning raw data to uncovering insights and creating data visualizations—to understand how different user segments engage with Cyclistic’s bikes. This exploration sheds light on the strategic opportunities for boosting membership, enhancing marketing efforts, and driving sustainable growth.
As you explore this case study, you’ll see how data-driven decision-making can spark powerful innovations in mobility and customer engagement. From analyzing ride-duration patterns to identifying potential marketing strategies, my goal is to show how careful research and thoughtful analysis can help businesses thrive in competitive environments. Enjoy the journey, and feel free to connect with me to discuss ideas and insights!

  • The Cyclistic member used classic bike more that other rideable type and more that the casual.
  • The top 3 month in frequency of ride in the 2024 were: August (12.9%), July (12.9%) and September (12.79%).
  • the top month for The Casual riders are: July (15.28%), August (15.06%) and September (14.23%).
    • The probability to find casual riders to convert are higher on those month according to our data form 2024.
  • The top month for The Cyclistic member are: September (11.99%), August (11.69%) and July (11.55%).
  • In this 2024 the day when the bikes were more use are: Saturday (15.44%), Wednesday(15.07%) and Friday(14.24%).
  • The top day when we can find The Casual riders according to the data form 2024 are: Saturday (20.88%) , Sunday (17.49%) and Friday (14.63%) .
    • Confirming that the casual riders are more frequent on weekends.
  • The top day when we can find The Cyclistic member according to the data form 2024 are: Wednesday (16.63%), Tuesday (15.09%) and Thursday (15.42%).
    • Confirming that the members are more frequent on week days.
  • The to pick in 2024 were we had more activity were: 8:00 am and 17:00 (5:00 pm).
  • The higher pick for both customer are the same time 17:00 (5:00pm) which make sense because sun is going down and the finish of work day for many users.
  • While the casual user have a consta increase on user having their higher pick on 17:00; the members have a two noticiable pick one on the begging of the working hour and at the end of it.
